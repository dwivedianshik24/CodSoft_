Biography

Ratan Naval Tata (28 December 1937 – 9 October 2024) was an Indian industrialist and philanthropist. He served as the chairman of Tata Group and Tata Sons from 1991 to 2012 and held the position of interim chairman from October 2016 to February 2017. In 2000, he received the Padma Bhushan, and in 2008, the Padma Vibhushan, two of India's highest civilian honors.

He was also an accomplished businessman who led Tata Group to acquire globally renowned brands like Tetley, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus, thus shaping Tata Group's global presence.

Major Achievements

  • Revolutionized the Tata Group and made it a global entity
  • Received the Padma Vibhushan (2008) and Padma Bhushan (2000) for his contributions
  • Successfully oversaw the launch of the Tata Nano, the world’s cheapest car.
